Frequently Asked Questions

What is FlowBoard and how does it differ from Jira?

FlowBoard is an AI-powered project management platform built specifically for engineering teams. Unlike Jira, which requires extensive configuration and relies on manual processes, FlowBoard uses artificial intelligence to automate sprint planning, predict delivery dates, and optimize team workflows. FlowBoard sets up in 5 minutes compared to Jira's typical 2-4 week onboarding process. The AI engine continuously learns from your team's patterns and proactively suggests improvements.

How accurate are FlowBoard's delivery predictions?

FlowBoard's predictive analytics engine achieves 94% accuracy on delivery date forecasts based on analysis of over 2 million completed sprints across 12,000 teams. The Science Behind FlowBoard's AI

FlowBoard's prediction engine is built on a transformer-based model trained on anonymized data from over 2 million completed sprints. The model processes 50+ input signals to generate delivery forecasts:

  • Velocity Signals: Story points completed per sprint, trend direction, and variance
  • Capacity Signals: Team availability, PTO schedules, on-call rotations, and meeting load
  • Complexity Signals: Task descriptions, labels, file change patterns, and dependency depth
  • External Signals: Sprint timing (end-of-quarter, holidays), company growth rate, and team tenure

The model outputs a probability distribution of completion dates, not just a single estimate. This allows teams to plan with confidence intervals. For example, "85% chance of completing by March 15, 95% chance by March 22." This level of transparency helps engineering leaders make informed trade-off decisions with stakeholders.

FlowBoard's AI is continuously updated. We retrain the global model monthly with new anonymized sprint data. Each team's instance also runs a local fine-tuning loop that adapts predictions to team-specific patterns within 2-4 sprints of initial setup. Privacy is paramount: team-specific data never leaves the local model, and the global model only receives statistical aggregates, never raw task data.
